Our Speakers

Meet the distinguished mathematicians and researchers presenting at the conference

Keynote Speakers

We are honored to welcome these distinguished mathematicians as our keynote speakers for the Mathematics Department Annual Talks 2025.

Dr. Emily Chen

Stanford University

Dr. Chen is a leading researcher in algebraic geometry with groundbreaking work on birational geometry and minimal models. Her research has been recognized with numerous awards, including the Fields Medal in 2022. At the conference, she will be presenting on "Advances in Algebraic Geometry and Applications."

Prof. Hiroshi Tanaka

University of Tokyo

Prof. Tanaka's work on geometric measure theory has revolutionized our understanding of fractal geometry and its applications. His keynote presentation on "Geometric Measure Theory and Applications" will explore recent breakthroughs and their implications for diverse fields from physics to finance.

Prof. David Rodriguez

Princeton University

As one of the world's foremost number theorists, Prof. Rodriguez has made significant contributions to our understanding of prime numbers and zeta functions. His keynote, "Modern Approaches to the Riemann Hypothesis," will present cutting-edge research on one of mathematics' most famous unsolved problems.

Distinguished Speakers

Our distinguished speakers represent leading institutions and research centers from around the world, bringing diverse perspectives and expertise to the conference.

Prof. Michael Davis

MIT

Leading researcher in combinatorial topology with applications in network theory and data analysis. His presentation on "Topological Data Analysis: Theory and Applications" will demonstrate how algebraic topology can be used to understand complex, high-dimensional data.

Dr. Sarah Patel

Oxford University

Expert in number theory and cryptography with significant contributions to elliptic curve cryptosystems. Her talk on "Recent Developments in Elliptic Curve Cryptography" will explore cutting-edge security applications in the digital age.

Prof. Richard Wong

UCLA

Renowned for his work in harmonic analysis and wavelets, Prof. Wong's research has applications in signal processing and image compression. His presentation will focus on new developments in time-frequency analysis.

Dr. Priya Sharma

IBM Research

At the intersection of mathematics and quantum computing, Dr. Sharma's work is pushing the boundaries of what's possible with quantum algorithms. Her talk will explore the mathematical foundations underlying quantum computation.

Prof. Thomas Meyer

ETH Zurich

Specializing in stochastic processes and financial mathematics, Prof. Meyer's research has transformed how we understand market dynamics and risk assessment. His presentation will cover recent advances in stochastic modeling for financial applications.

Dr. Carlos Fernandez

University of Barcelona

Dr. Fernandez has made significant contributions to numerical methods for solving differential equations, with applications in fluid dynamics and climate modeling. His talk will present innovative computational approaches to complex differential systems.

Dr. Alex Kim

Google Research

Working at the intersection of mathematics and machine learning, Dr. Kim's research focuses on optimization algorithms and tensor methods for deep learning. His presentation will explore current mathematical frontiers in AI research.

Prof. Lisa Johnson

University of Waterloo

Prof. Johnson's work in combinatorial game theory has provided new insights into perfect information games and their mathematical structures. Her talk will present recent developments in the analysis of combinatorial games and their strategic depth.

Dr. Anita Desai

Educational Technology Institute

As a mathematician specializing in education research, Dr. Desai bridges the gap between abstract mathematics and effective teaching methodologies. She will be moderating the panel discussion on "Mathematics Education in the Digital Age."

Panel Discussion

Mathematics Education in the Digital Age

Moderator: Dr. Anita Desai, Educational Technology Institute

This panel will explore how digital technologies are transforming mathematics education at all levels, from primary school to university research. Panelists will discuss innovative pedagogical approaches, online learning platforms, and the future of mathematics education in a connected world.

Panelists:

  • • Prof. Michael Davis, MIT
  • • Dr. Sarah Patel, Oxford University
  • • Prof. Rajiv Kumar, Head of Mathematics Department
  • • Dr. Lisa Johnson, University of Waterloo

Time: Day 1 (May 15), 16:00 - 17:00

Location: Room 301
